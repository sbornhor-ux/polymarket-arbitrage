Term Definitions

Composite Score
Blended signal: 40% LLM Relevance Score + 60% Statistical Correlation. Higher = stronger overall signal.
Statistical Correlation in Historical Movement
Statistical similarity between Polymarket probability changes and financial returns, combining correlation, Granger causality, lead-lag CCF, event study, and volatility spillover tests. 0–1 scale.
LLM Relevance Score
LLM-assessed likelihood (1–100) that this financial instrument meaningfully responds to the Polymarket outcome. 90+ = directly named company or instrument. 10–29 = tenuous connection. Below 10 = essentially guessing.
Alignment
With: instrument price rises when Polymarket YES probability rises. Against: instrument price falls when YES probability rises (inverse relationship).
Discovery
Which market incorporated information first. Poly leads: Polymarket priced it before financial markets — potential trading edge. Fin leads: financial markets moved first — Polymarket odds may lag. Simultaneous: both markets moved together.
Pearson r
Linear correlation coefficient between Polymarket probability changes and financial returns over the aligned window. Range −1 to +1. Values near ±1 indicate strong co-movement.
n obs
Number of aligned hourly observations used in the statistical analysis. More observations = more reliable stats. Below 20 may produce unreliable results.
